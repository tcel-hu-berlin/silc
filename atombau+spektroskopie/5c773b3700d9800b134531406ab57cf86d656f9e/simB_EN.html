Atomic energy levels and line spectra

Simulation B: Spectrum of hydrogen

Overview:

  • Simulation B shows the energy levels of a hydrogen atom according to the Bohr model.
  • The red   +  sign denotes the nucleus of the atom, while the blue   -  sign denotes an electron.
  • The start and end positions of the electron can be adjusted through dragging, simulating various transitions between the energy levels.
  • The wavelength of light corresponding to each transition is shown along the black wavelength axis (above the simulation's gray box). The energy (in eV) and wavelength (in nm) of each transition are also displayed between the start and end positions of the electron.

Note:

  • For simplicity, the energy shells are shown to be equidistant in the simulation. In reality, the radius of each shell depends on the square of the principal quantum number n of each level.

Tips:

  • The energy values of the energy levels with n = 2 – 8 (which you can determine from the simulation) can be entered in a table in the "Table" tab. The energy of the first level, E(n = 1) = –13.6 eV, is already provided.
  • You can only enter numbers in the table; equations are not accepted!
  • If you have entered everything correctly, your values should appear as points in the graph next to the table.
  • In the tab "Algebra" (calculator symbol), you can use slilders for the variables a and b to determine an equation of the form E(x)=a·xb that best connects the points plotted.
