Atomic energy levels and line spectra

Animation A: Principles of photoelectron spectroscopy

Photoelectron spectroscopy (PES) uses UV light or X-rays to remove electrons from different energy levels of a substance. The removal of an electron (ionization) occurs when the energy of the light is sufficient to excite the electron from its original energy level (E < 0) to the vacuum energy (E = 0). If the kinetic energy of the removed electrons is measured (and the energy of the light is known), then the "binding energy" of the different energy levels of the atom can be determined. The peaks in a PES spectrum therefore represent the different energy levels (shells and their subshells) of a sample and contain information about both the energy and the number of electrons in the subshells.
