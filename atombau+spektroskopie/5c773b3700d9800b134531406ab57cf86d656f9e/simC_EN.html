Simulation C: Visualizing quantum numbers

The principal quantum number n ist not the only quantum number that is important for describing the energy and location of electrons around atoms. Electrons can exist in a variety of atomic orbitals with different size, shape, and orientation. To better understand these qualities (and the quantum numbers that represent them), you can use Simulation C, which was developed by Paul Falstad and which beautifully depicts the orbitals of hydrogen-like atoms.

Overview:

  • You can choose a combination of the quantum numbers n, l and ml and view the wave function of the corresponding atomic orbital.
  • If desired, the 3D view of the orbital can be rotated by dragging on the screen.
  • The changing colors on the orbitals represent the phase changes of their wave functions, a topic that falls outside of the scope of a typical general chemistry course.
