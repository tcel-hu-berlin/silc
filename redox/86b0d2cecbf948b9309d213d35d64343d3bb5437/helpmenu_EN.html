Balancing redox equationsShow

The following steps allow one to balance redox equations without using oxidation states. Taking the pair MnO4-/Mn2+ as an example:

  • Balance all elements except for H and O:
    (Mn atoms are already balanced)
  • Balance the number of O by adding water molecules:
    MnO4- → Mn2+ + 4H2O
  • Balance the number of H by adding protons (or hydronium ions and water):
    MnO4- + 8 H+ → Mn2+ + 4 H2O
    (MnO4- + 8 H3O+→ Mn2+ + 12 H2O)
  • Add electrons to the appropriate side in order to achieve a charge balance:
    MnO4- + 8 H+ + 5 e-→ Mn2+ + 4 H2O
    (MnO4- + 8 H3O+ + 5 e-→ Mn2+ + 12 H2O)

In case the reaction occurs in basic medium, add as many hydroxide ions to both sides as there are protons or hydronium ions:
MnO4- + 8 H+ + 8 OH- + 5 e- → Mn2+ + 4 H2O + 8 OH-
MnO4- + 8 H2O + 5 e-→ Mn2+ + 4 H2O + 8 OH-
MnO4- + 4 H2O + 5 e-→ Mn2+ + 8 OH-

Preparation Question 4eShow

When determining the cell voltage, the standard potentials are subtracted from each other without multiplication, even if their corresponding equations in (e) were multiplied by an integer.

Question 4Show

Very often, real galvanic cells are build such that the + side of the voltmeter is connected to the cathode and the - side with the cathode, in order to obtain a positive voltage reading. Because of this, the formula ΔEcell = Ecathode- Eanode is sometimes used. However, if you use this equation, you should keep in mind that the "cathode" is the actual cathode if and only if the cell potential ΔEcell is positive!
